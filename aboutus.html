About Us

Introduction

The Lions Club is an international service organization dedicated to fostering a spirit of community service and addressing pressing global challenges. Established in 1917, it has grown to include over 1.4 million members in more than 200 countries and geographic areas. The organization focuses on key areas such as combating vision problems, alleviating hunger, supporting youth programs, addressing environmental concerns, and providing disaster relief. Lions Clubs operate at the local level, allowing members to address the specific needs of their communities while contributing to larger global initiatives.
Guided by the motto "We Serve," the Lions Club promotes leadership, volunteerism, and a commitment to making a positive impact on society.

History

The Lions Club was founded in 1917 by Melvin Jones, a Chicago-based insurance agent who believed that individuals should use their talents to improve their communities and the world. His vision led to the creation of a service-focused organization, which quickly gained traction. The first Lions Club meeting was held in Dallas, Texas, and within a few years, it expanded beyond the United States to become an international movement. A pivotal moment in Lions Club history came in 1925, when Helen Keller addressed the International Convention and challenged members to become "knights of the blind in the crusade against darkness." This call to action inspired Lions to make vision care and blindness prevention a cornerstone of their mission, a commitment that continues today. Over the decades, the Lions Club has grown into one of the largest and most influential service organizations in the world.
